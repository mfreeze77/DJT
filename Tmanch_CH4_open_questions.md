# Chapter 4 Open Questions and Records Plan

## Purpose

This file converts Chapter 4’s strongest six-degrees leads into records programs. Each program identifies:

- the unresolved question;
- the hypothesis being tested;
- records and custodians;
- relevant dates and search terms;
- the proof threshold;
- priority; and
- where useful, draft request language.

A recurring person or entity is not treated as proof of coordination. The objective is to find the transaction, communication, or instruction that connects the nodes—or to establish that no such connection appears in the available record.

## Draft FOIA module

```text

subjects, geographic areas, business entities, or transaction categories
included in Felix Sater’s officially acknowledged cooperation, limited to

identifying whether Bayrock Group, Trump-branded real-estate projects,
Russia, Kazakhstan, or the City of Almaty/BTA Bank matters were included.

information whose disclosure could endanger any person.
```

# Evidentiary standard

A six-degrees chain becomes a meaningful finding only when at least two adjacent links are supported by primary records and the proposed reciprocity is tied to a transaction, communication, or decision.

Examples:

- **Weak:** Person A attended the same event as Person B.
- **Moderate:** Person A introduced Buyer B and received a commission.
- **Strong:** Buyer B’s contracts satisfied a lender threshold, and Person A received a separate project benefit documented in contemporaneous communications.
- **Very strong:** Funds, instructions, and reciprocal benefits are traced through bank records, contracts, and communications.

The research should preserve hunches aggressively while refusing to convert resemblance or proximity into proof before the connecting documents are obtained.
