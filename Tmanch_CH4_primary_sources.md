# Chapter 4 Primary-Source and Documentary Inventory

## Purpose

This inventory identifies the records used to expand Chapter 4 and states the proper evidentiary use and limitations of each. “Primary” includes court opinions, pleadings, regulatory findings, official reports, company disclosures, recorded property records, contracts, and contemporaneous statements. High-quality investigative reporting is separately labeled where the underlying record is not yet in the repository.

---

## PS4-001 — *Kriss v. BayRock Group LLC*, SDNY Document 439

- **Court:** U.S. District Court, Southern District of New York
- **Date:** December 2, 2016
- **Case:** No. 1:10-cv-03959
- **Location:** https://law.justia.com/cases/federal/district-courts/new-york/nysdce/1%3A2010cv03959/362805/439/
- **Type:** Judicial opinion on motions to dismiss
- **Supports:** Bayrock entity structure; project-specific LLCs; plaintiffs’ description of FL Group transaction; projected profits; alleged loan/sale characterization; RICO and tax allegations that survived or failed at pleading stage.
- **Does not support:** A final finding that Bayrock committed tax fraud, that all allegations were true, that Trump knew the alleged structure, or that FL funds were Russian state money.
- **Reliability note:** Factual allegations were accepted as true for pleading purposes. The litigation later settled rather than producing a complete merits judgment.

## Source hierarchy applied

1. **Judicial and official findings:** PS4-001, 002, 007, 009, 011, 012, 015, 020, 021.
2. **Contemporaneous company disclosure:** PS4-003.
3. **Pleadings and settlements:** PS4-004, 005, 006, 008, 010.
4. **Documentary investigative reporting:** PS4-013, 016, 017, 018, 019.
5. **Missing primary records explicitly targeted:** PS4-022 through PS4-030.

No claim should be upgraded from allegation to fact merely because it appears in several secondary articles that trace back to the same complaint or unnamed source.
