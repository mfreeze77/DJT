# Donald Trump Financial Network, 1983–1986: Atlantic City, Aviation, and Primary-Record Tranche

## Purpose

This tranche supplements:

- [`Tmanch_Fin_83-86_Assoc.md`](./Tmanch_Fin_83-86_Assoc.md)
- [`Tmanch_Fin_83-86_PP.md`](./Tmanch_Fin_83-86_PP.md)

It converts the Atlantic City and aviation portions of the existing research into a primary-record plan. The goal is to determine what federal investigators and New Jersey casino regulators documented about land assembly, lessors, beneficial owners, vendor suitability, aviation services, financial flows, and law-enforcement referrals.

This document does **not** assume that every person with a criminal association transmitted criminal proceeds to a Trump entity. It identifies records that could establish or rebut a specific transaction-level connection.

## Principal record clusters

### 1. S&G Associates, Daniel Sullivan, and Kenneth Shapiro

The existing associate tranche identifies S&G Associates, Daniel Sullivan, and Kenneth Shapiro as important to Atlantic City parcel assembly and casino development. The core factual questions are:

- Which parcels were bought, leased, assigned, or sold?
- What were the acquisition and disposition prices?
- Who supplied the purchase money?
- Who received sale, lease, option, or buyout proceeds?
- Did any party act as a nominee or undisclosed beneficial owner?
- What did New Jersey regulators learn about the parties' backgrounds?
- Did regulators require a lease buyout, restructuring, divestiture, or enhanced disclosure?
- Which federal or state agencies supplied information to the regulators?

### 2. Trump Plaza / Harrah's and Trump-affiliated Atlantic City entities

Potentially responsive entities and project names include:

- Trump Plaza Associates.
- Harrah's at Trump Plaza.
- Trump Plaza Hotel and Casino.
- Harrah's Associates.
- Trump Organization entities appearing in applications or contracts.
- Holiday Inns, Harrah's, and related operating or financing entities.

The relevant records may include license applications, qualification reports, hearing exhibits, land and lease schedules, lender disclosures, vendor files, beneficial-ownership certifications, final agency findings, and correspondence with law enforcement.

### 3. Joseph Weichselbaum, Damin Aviation, and Damin Leasing

The existing Chapter 2 support material identifies Joseph Weichselbaum and aviation services provided to Trump-affiliated casino operations as a separate research cluster. The primary questions are:

- Which Damin entity contracted with which casino or Trump entity?
- What services, aircraft, routes, passengers, and payment arrangements appear in records?
- When did regulators or casino officials learn of the federal narcotics investigation or indictment?
- Did a vendor qualification, waiver, exemption, or suitability review occur?
- Did federal investigators request contracts, invoices, aircraft records, or casino transportation records?
- Did sentencing, forfeiture, or asset-tracing materials identify business payments or property transactions involving Trump-affiliated entities?

The public appellate citation [`United States v. Weichselbaum`, 853 F.2d 923 (3d Cir. 1988)](https://law.justia.com/cases/federal/appellate-courts/F2/853/923/121325/) confirms a District of New Jersey prosecution and affirmance, but the underlying district-court docket and complete case file should be obtained before drawing conclusions.

## Why DGE and CCC requests must be separated

The Division of Gaming Enforcement and Casino Control Commission are distinct custodians. File one request with each. A request submitted to the wrong employee or custodian may not trigger the protections and deadlines of New Jersey's Open Public Records Act.

Official sources:

- [New Jersey OPRA Central](https://www.nj.gov/opra/)
- [NJ Casino Control Commission — OPRA](https://www.nj.gov/casinos/about/request/)
- [NJ Department of Law and Public Safety — OPRA](https://www.nj.gov/oag/opra/)

The Commission states that it ordinarily must grant, deny, or provide an availability estimate within seven business days after receiving a complete request, with different handling for stored or archived material.

## Record request 1 — S&G Associates / Sullivan / Shapiro

Submit substantially the same request separately to DGE and CCC.

```text
Subject: OPRA Request — S&G Associates, Sullivan, Shapiro, and
Trump Plaza land and casino-review records, 1981–1988

Under the New Jersey Open Public Records Act, I request electronic
copies of non-exempt government records dated January 1, 1981 through
December 31, 1988 concerning:

• S&G Associates
• Daniel Sullivan
• Kenneth Shapiro
• Trump Plaza Associates
• Harrah's at Trump Plaza
• Trump Plaza Hotel and Casino
• Land purchases, options, leases, lease assignments, lease buyouts,
  parcel assembly, or beneficial ownership involving those parties in
  Atlantic City

This request is limited to the following identifiable record classes:

1. Casino-license, qualification, and suitability applications,
   supplements, schedules, and final determinations.
2. Records reviewing land ownership, sellers, landlords, lessors,
   option holders, beneficial owners, financing sources, and persons
   receiving payments.
3. Final investigative reports and final agency findings.
4. Hearing exhibits, transcripts, agendas, minutes, orders, and staff
   reports.
5. Records submitted by an applicant or licensee concerning the named
   parties or transactions.
6. Correspondence with the FBI, DEA, IRS, a United States Attorney's
   Office, New Jersey State Police, or another law-enforcement agency.
7. Records concerning any requirement or recommendation that a lease,
   option, ownership interest, or payment arrangement be terminated,
   bought out, restructured, or further disclosed because of regulatory
   or suitability concerns.
8. Existing indexes, file inventories, box lists, accession records, or
   retention-transfer records identifying responsive archived files.

I do not seek Social Security numbers, tax returns, medical records,
fingerprints, confidential-source identities, protected home addresses,
or information whose release is specifically prohibited by law.

Please produce records electronically and on a rolling basis. If a
record is withheld in part, please release the non-exempt portion and
identify the legal basis for each redaction or withholding.
```

## Transaction-level evidence matrix

| Question | Best supporting record | What it can establish |
|---|---|---|
| Who owned or controlled a parcel? | Deed, option, lease, corporate filing, regulator schedule | Legal or disclosed beneficial interest |
| Who supplied acquisition funds? | Mortgage, closing statement, bank record in court exhibit, financial investigation | Source or route of funds |
| Who received markup or lease proceeds? | Closing statement, contract, agency exhibit, tax or forfeiture record | Recipient and amount |
| Did regulators object? | Final report, hearing transcript, order, minutes | Official concern and required remedy |
| Did law enforcement share information? | Agency correspondence, referral, final investigative report | Interagency transmission and subject matter |
| Did casino entities keep using a vendor after notice? | Contracts, invoices, board minutes, regulator correspondence | Timing and institutional knowledge |
| Did a narcotics investigation identify the same asset or company? | DEA/FBI asset report, forfeiture filing, court exhibit | Exact investigative intersection |

## Evidence controls

### Distinguish procedural stages

- An **investigation** is not a charge.
- An **indictment** is an allegation.
- A **guilty plea or verdict** establishes only the offenses adjudicated.
- A **regulatory concern** is not necessarily a criminal finding.
- A **business relationship** is not proof that one party knew the full background or source of funds of another.

### Use period-correct financial terminology

The principal federal money-laundering offenses in 18 U.S.C. §§ 1956 and 1957 were enacted in 1986. Earlier records may be indexed under:

- Financial investigation.
- Asset tracing.
- Concealment of proceeds.
- Currency transactions.
- Nominee ownership.
- Tax referral.
- Forfeiture or seizure.
- Unexplained cash.
- Racketeering proceeds.
- Narcotics proceeds.

Do not limit pre-1986 searches to the modern phrase “money laundering.”

Official background: [DOJ Criminal Resource Manual — Money Laundering, 18 U.S.C. §§ 1956 and 1957](https://www.justice.gov/archives/usam/criminal-resource-manual-957-money-laundering-18-usc-1956-1957)

## Relationship to the Cuba-drug hypothesis

No document presently cited in this tranche establishes that Cuban-linked narcotics proceeds entered an Atlantic City Trump project. The proper method is to obtain the Cuba-route investigation's names, aliases, companies, addresses, vessels, aircraft, accounts, attorneys, and file numbers, then compare those identifiers against Atlantic City property, aviation, casino-regulatory, and investigative records.

A connection becomes materially significant only when independent primary records share a sufficiently specific identifier.
