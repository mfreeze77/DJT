# Chapter 5 Primary-Source Inventory

## Source-control standard

Each entry records what the source establishes and what it cannot establish by itself. Indictments are allegations. Guilty pleas establish admitted facts and offenses. Jury verdicts establish the charged offenses proved at trial. Congressional reports, intelligence assessments, inspector-general reports, sanctions designations, and constitutional proceedings each have different legal and evidentiary meanings.

## Commercial conflict and Trump Tower Moscow

- **Agency/Court:** U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York; Special Counsel referral
- **Date:** November 29, 2018
- **Case:** *United States v. Michael Cohen*, false statements to Congress

- **Supports:** Moscow project chronology; Russian interference; campaign contacts; WikiLeaks interest; June 9 meeting; Manafort/Kilimnik; Papadopoulos; transition contacts; investigation limitations.
- **Does not establish:** A campaign–Russian government criminal conspiracy or coordination agreement.

### CH5-SRC-003 — October 28, 2015 Trump Tower Moscow letter of intent

- **Record type:** Executed commercial document reproduced in congressional and investigative records
- **Participants:** Donald Trump; Michael Cohen; Russian counterparties
- **Supports:** Trump personally approved a campaign-period Moscow development proposal.
- **Does not establish:** Final financing, governmental approval, construction, or payment.
- **Retrieval target:** Certified complete copy with all schedules, drafts, signatures, and transmission metadata.

### CH5-SRC-004 — Sater–Cohen campaign-period communications

- **Record type:** Emails and text messages reproduced in congressional and Special Counsel materials
- **Date:** 2015–2016
