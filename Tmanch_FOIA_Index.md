## Research architecture

The research is deliberately divided into three independent record trails:

| Trail | Core question | Initial record sources |
|---|---|---|
| Property and financing | Who acquired, financed, owned, transferred, or benefited from the relevant property interests? | ACRIS, deeds, mortgages, leases, public-agency files, court records |
| Organized crime, aviation, and casino regulation | What did investigators and regulators document about contractors, land intermediaries, vendors, and aviation services? | FBI, DEA, EOUSA, DGE, CCC, federal court files |
| Cuba-linked narcotics route | What people, entities, assets, vessels, aircraft, accounts, and routes were identified in the federal investigation? | S.D. Florida court file, DEA, EOUSA, FBI, State, CIA, Customs successors, Coast Guard |

Only after each trail has been independently documented should identifiers be compared across trails.

## Evidence rule

A meaningful intersection requires a sufficiently specific shared identifier, such as:

- The same legal entity.
- The same person with corroborating biographical identifiers.
- The same address, telephone number, account, attorney, or title company.
- The same vessel or aircraft registration.
- The same property unit or parcel.
- An explicit agency cross-reference or related-case number.

Temporal overlap, geographic proximity, a similar surname, or a later unrelated conviction is not enough.

## FOIA drafting rules

### Ask for records, not answers

A valid request seeks existing agency records. FOIA does not require an agency to conduct research, analyze data, answer questions, or create a new chronology.

Prefer:

> Existing final investigative summaries, asset-identification records, interagency correspondence, and records identifying case numbers concerning [defined event and dates].

Avoid:

> Was Donald Trump connected to Cuban drug trafficking?

Official source: [FOIA.gov — How to Make a FOIA Request](https://www.foia.gov/how-to.html)

### Reasonably describe the search

Supply:

- Exact case caption and number.
- Date range.
- Names and aliases.
- Locations.
- Companies.
- Vessels and aircraft.
- Field offices likely to hold records.
- Specific record categories.

### Separate agency components

DOJ components process their own records. Send independent requests to FBI, DEA, EOUSA, and any other component likely to hold responsive material.

Official source: [DOJ — Make a FOIA Request](https://www.justice.gov/oip/make-foia-request-doj)

### Protect against third-party privacy denials

For living people, center requests on a public prosecution, transaction, organization, historical event, or official agency action. A request for another living person's unrestricted law-enforcement file will commonly trigger a response that neither confirms nor denies responsive records unless consent, proof of death, official acknowledgment, or a sufficiently strong public interest is supplied.

When requesting a deceased person's file, attach acceptable proof of death.

Official sources:

- [DOJ — Make a FOIA Request](https://www.justice.gov/oip/make-foia-request-doj)
- [FBI — Requesting FBI Records](https://www.fbi.gov/how-we-can-help-you/more-fbi-services-and-information/freedom-of-information-privacy-act/requesting-fbi-records)
- [DEA — Make a Request](https://www.dea.gov/foia/requesting-foia-records)

### Use period-correct financial terms

For records before the federal money-laundering statutes enacted in 1986, include terms such as:

- Financial investigation.
- Asset tracing.
- Concealment of proceeds.
- Currency transaction.
- Nominee ownership.
- Tax referral.
- Forfeiture or seizure.
- Unexplained cash.
- Racketeering or narcotics proceeds.

Do not search only for the later statutory phrase “money laundering.”

## Common federal-request footer

```text
Search and format instructions

Please search all record systems and locations reasonably likely to
contain responsive records, including headquarters, relevant field
offices, legacy indices, retired records, cross-references, and records
retrieved from a Federal Records Center.

If responsive records originated with or are properly maintained by a
different agency or component, please refer the records or the relevant
portion of this request to that component and identify the referral.

Please produce records electronically in searchable PDF format. Where a
record is maintained as a spreadsheet, database export, email, or other
native electronic format, please preserve that format where reasonably
feasible.

Please make rolling or interim releases rather than holding all
responsive records until processing is complete.

Please release every reasonably segregable non-exempt portion of each
responsive record and identify the exemption relied upon for each
withholding or redaction.

If the agency believes the scope is unduly burdensome, please contact me
before administratively closing the request. I am willing to discuss
prioritizing final reports, closing summaries, financial or asset
records, interagency correspondence, and existing case-number records
as an initial tranche.

Fees

I agree to pay properly assessed fees up to $50 without further
authorization. Please notify me before incurring fees above that amount.
```

## Fee-waiver language

```text
I request a waiver or reduction of fees.

I am an independent, noncommercial researcher preparing a publicly
accessible historical analysis of federal investigative and regulatory
activity concerning narcotics trafficking, organized crime, financial
investigations, casino regulation, and real-property asset tracing in
the late 1970s and 1980s.

The requested records will shed light on identifiable operations and
activities of government agencies, including how agencies investigated
an international narcotics route, traced proceeds and assets,
coordinated with casino regulators, and communicated across agency
boundaries.

I intend to analyze responsive records, publish relevant records and
source citations in a freely accessible public research repository, and
provide explanatory historical context. The request is not primarily in
my commercial interest.

If a complete fee waiver is not granted, please process the request
subject to the fee limit stated above.
```

A fee waiver is not automatic. The submission should explain how the records will improve public understanding of identifiable government operations and how the requester will disseminate the information.

## Response and appeal controls

- The federal statutory response period generally concerns an agency's determination, not guaranteed production of all records within twenty working days.
- Track every clarification, fee issue, referral, and production date.
- Preserve envelopes, emails, and portal correspondence.
- For DOJ components, an administrative appeal generally must be transmitted or postmarked within 90 calendar days of the component response.
- Submit one appeal per request and identify the component, request number, response date, and challenged determination.

Official sources:

- [FOIA statute](https://www.foia.gov/foia-statute.html)
- [DOJ — Submit and Track a Request or Appeal](https://www.justice.gov/oip/submit-and-track-request-or-appeal)

## New Jersey OPRA controls

- Submit DGE and CCC requests to their respective custodians.
- Use the required state or agency request form.
- Ask for identifiable record classes rather than demanding that the custodian research or explain events.
- Request electronic records and rolling production.
- Ask for the legal basis for each denial or redaction.
- Track the seven-business-day response or availability-notice period and any agreed extensions.

Official sources:

- [New Jersey OPRA Central](https://www.nj.gov/opra/)
- [NJ Casino Control Commission — OPRA](https://www.nj.gov/casinos/about/request/)
- [NJ Government Records Council](https://www.nj.gov/grc/public/)

## Production processing workflow

For each production:

1. Preserve the original file and its delivery metadata.
2. Assign a local production identifier.
3. Record agency, request number, production number, and page range.
4. Create a document inventory before interpretation.
5. Extract names and aliases exactly as written.
6. Extract companies, addresses, telephone numbers, accounts, vessels, aircraft, properties, and file numbers.
7. Record every redaction and exemption.
8. Separate agency statements from witness allegations and press material.
9. Cross-check important facts against the court file or another primary source.
10. Update the match table using exact, probable, possible, or rejected classifications.

## Publication language

Use wording that reflects the evidence stage:

- “The indictment alleged …”
- “An FBI report recorded …”
- “A witness stated …”
- “The regulator found …”
- “The court held …”
- “The defendant pleaded guilty to …”
- “The records do not establish …”

Avoid collapsing allegations, intelligence reporting, agency suspicions, regulatory concerns, and judicial findings into one category.

## Current bottom line

The public record supplies multiple promising acquisition paths, but it does not yet establish that proceeds from the Guillot-Lara route or another Cuba-linked narcotics operation were laundered through a Trump property. The new tranches are designed to test that proposition with transaction-level evidence and to document negative results just as carefully as positive intersections.
