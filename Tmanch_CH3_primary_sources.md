# Chapter 3 Primary-Source Inventory

## Recovery capital, public risk transfer, foreign partnerships, presales, and post-Soviet intermediaries

## How to use this inventory

This inventory distinguishes between:

- **Primary records**: court decisions, SEC filings, official enforcement actions, recorded-property systems, statutes, regulations, and government reports.
- **Contemporaneous transaction reporting**: reporting published when a transaction occurred and ordinarily based on identified participants or documents.
- **Documentary investigative reporting**: later reporting that identifies underlying records, named investigators, mortgages, deeds, or law-enforcement reports.
- **Lead sources**: material useful for locating records but insufficient by itself to establish a serious allegation.

Each entry states both what a source supports and what it does not support. A source describing a transaction does not automatically establish a hidden motive, criminal knowledge, intelligence direction, or reciprocal agreement.

# S01 — Trump Hotels & Casino Resorts 1996 Form 10-K

**Source type:** SEC filing; primary corporate record  
**Entity:** Trump Hotels & Casino Resorts, Inc.  
**Filed:** 1997, reporting fiscal year ended December 31, 1996  
**Stable location:** [SEC filing text](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/943320/0000940180-97-000299.txt)

## Supports

- Trump Plaza’s 1992 restructuring terms.
- The 1993 Plaza refinancing through $330 million of mortgage notes and $60 million of PIK notes and warrants.
- The 1995 THCR common-stock and secured-note offerings.
- The 1996 common-stock offering and $1.2 billion first-mortgage-note issuance.
- Acquisition of the Taj and Castle interests.
- Trump’s receipt of interests convertible into 5,837,700 THCR shares in the Castle transaction.
- The $168.126 million assigned value of Trump’s contribution.
- Negative book value of the acquired Castle entity.
- Purchase accounting recording approximately $196.109 million above the fair value of acquired net assets.
- Trump’s increased beneficial ownership.
- Shareholder derivative allegations and the company’s denial.
- Stock-price ranges.
- Casino marketing, credit, complimentary benefits, international representatives, player tracking, and high-end customer systems.

## Does not support

- A finding that the Castle transaction was legally fraudulent.
- A finding that public investors knowingly financed a criminal scheme.
- A finding that any particular casino customer laundered money.
- A conclusion that Trump personally directed every operational decision.

## Reliability limits

The filing is authoritative for disclosed corporate facts and management’s representations. It is not neutral on disputed litigation allegations. Use the underlying prospectuses, contribution agreements, appraisals, board records, and court dispositions for contested matters.

# S03 — *Uzan v. 845 UN Limited Partnership*

**Source type:** Published New York appellate decision  
**Court:** Appellate Division, First Department  
**Date:** June 17, 2004  
**Stable locations:**

- [New York State Law Reporting Bureau](https://www.nycourts.gov/Reporter/3dseries/2004/2004_05172.htm)
- [Justia reproduction](https://law.justia.com/cases/new-york/appellate-division-first-department/2004/2004-05172.html)

## Supports

- Trump World Tower sales beginning in October 1998.
- Donald Trump’s role as managing general partner of the sponsor.
- Seven preconstruction purchase agreements signed in April 1999.
- Four penthouse purchases at an aggregate negotiated price of approximately $32 million.
- A discount exceeding $7 million from offering-plan prices.
- Staged deposits totaling 25%.

- Nonstandard rights to advertise, assign, and resell before closing.

- A finding that the buyers’ deposits were directly spent on construction.
- A finding that the buyers acted for a foreign government or criminal organization.
- A finding that Trump knew of later fraud findings involving the Uzan family.
- A finding that discounts or assignment rights were unlawful.

## Reliability limits

The decision resolves contract and litigation issues arising after the purchases. The complete offering plan, contracts, escrow agreement, broker records, source-of-funds files, and construction-loan covenants are still required.

# S30 — Master recovery cash-flow ledger

**Source type:** Derived analytical dataset

## Purpose

The final Chapter 3 financial conclusion should be based on annual inflows, debt relief, liability assumptions, guarantees released, equity transferred, and cash retained—not on isolated anecdotes.

## Required columns

- date;
- Trump entity;
- counterparty;
- transaction type;
- gross amount;
- net cash to Trump entity;
- debt assumed or discharged;
- guarantee released;
- ownership surrendered;
- ownership retained;
- source country;
- beneficial owner;
- source tier;
- evidence status;
- link to underlying document.

## Limitation
