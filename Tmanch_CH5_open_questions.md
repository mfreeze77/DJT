# Chapter 5 Open Questions and Records Plan

## Purpose

The public record is unusually rich but incomplete. This file turns unresolved claims into document-specific research programs. Each program identifies the proposition, custodians, date range, search terms, proof threshold, and a filing module.

## Access note for first-term presidential records

The National Archives states that Donald Trump’s first-term Presidential records became subject to FOIA under the Presidential Records Act on **January 20, 2026**. PRA restrictions and FOIA exemptions still apply, and classified records may require Mandatory Declassification Review.

- [NARA Presidential Records Act overview](https://www.archives.gov/news/topics/presidential-records-act)
- [Accessing Presidential collections](https://www.archives.gov/presidential-records/research/accessing-the-collections)
- **Donald J. Trump Presidential Library contact:** `trump.library@nara.gov`

Presidential records should be requested from NARA, not treated as ordinary agency FOIA records. Agency-created records at State, Treasury, DOD, OMB, FBI, and other components remain subject to each agency’s own FOIA process.

# Evidence threshold for the project’s strongest hypothesis

A finding of formal Russian control would require substantially more than policy alignment or favorable rhetoric. The strongest evidence would include one or more of:

- recruitment or handling documentation;
- tasking and compliance communications;
- covert compensation;
- threats using concealed financial or personal information;
- policy instructions linked to benefits;
- intelligence-source corroboration from independent channels;
- or participant testimony supported by records.

The current public evidence establishes a broad vulnerability and repeated exploitation. The records programs above are designed to determine whether any specific channel crosses from access and influence into direction, exchange, or control.
