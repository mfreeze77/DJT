# Chapter 1 Open Questions and Records Plan

The expansion produced stronger evidence, but also identified records capable of resolving the most important remaining questions.

## Priority scale

- **P0:** Could materially establish or disprove orchestration, recruitment, or a direct intelligence link.
- **P1:** Could resolve a major chronology or identity inconsistency.
- **P2:** Important context or corroboration.

# 11. How unusual was her case among legal emigrants rather than defectors?

**Priority:** P2

## Questions

- How often did Czechoslovak women marry Austrians and retain return access?
- Were athlete emigrants treated differently from ordinary emigrants?
- Did the StB routinely pressure parents of legal emigrants for information?
- Did other expatriates who married influential Westerners receive similar operational attention?

## Method

Build a matched comparison group rather than relying only on celebrity defectors:

- female Czechoslovak athletes or instructors;
- emigrated between 1968 and 1975;
- acquired Western status through marriage;
- retained family in Czechoslovakia;
- returned before 1989;
- and married or associated with influential Western figures.

## Research value

This can determine whether Ivana’s treatment was unique, unusual, or part of a broader StB exploitation practice.
