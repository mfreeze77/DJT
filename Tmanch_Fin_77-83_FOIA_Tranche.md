# Donald Trump Financial History, 1977–1983: Primary-Record and FOIA Research Tranche

## Purpose

This tranche extends [`Tmanch_Fin_77-83.md`](./Tmanch_Fin_77-83.md) with a primary-record acquisition plan. Its purpose is not to assume a criminal conclusion. It is to identify records capable of confirming, narrowing, or disproving claims about Donald J. Trump's early Manhattan financing, construction relationships, property transactions, and possible intersections with federal investigations.

The core research rule is:

> Treat a suspected connection as a hypothesis until a primary record supplies a shared person, company, address, account, aircraft, vessel, telephone number, property, or investigative file number.

This approach supports the repository's stated standard of separating facts from speculation.

## Existing public-record anchors

### 1. Grand Hyatt / Commodore redevelopment, 1977–1980

The existing financial tranche identifies the Commodore Hotel redevelopment as Trump's first major Manhattan project and describes the Hyatt partnership, government-assisted ownership and lease structure, tax abatement, construction financing, and Fred Trump's guarantees.

Primary records should be used to reconstruct:

- The acquisition and option agreements.
- The Urban Development Corporation ownership and lease structure.
- Appraisals and feasibility studies.
- Tax-abatement applications and approvals.
- Loan guarantees, mortgages, assignments, and satisfactions.
- Beneficial interests held by Trump, Hyatt, Fred Trump, and project entities.
- Public-agency communications concerning the transaction.

### 2. Trump Tower, 1978–1983

The existing research identifies Trump Tower as the central transaction in this period. The primary-record inquiry should separately reconstruct:

- Site acquisition and air-rights agreements.
- Equitable Life's ownership and project interest.
- Construction loans and mortgage instruments.
- Condominium declarations, offering plans, amendments, and initial unit sales.
- Contractors, subcontractors, labor entities, and payment relationships.
- Any federal subpoenas, interviews, investigations, or referrals concerning construction, labor, concrete, or unit transfers.

### 3. Concrete, labor, and organized-crime investigations

The repository's Chapter 2 support material identifies the following as subjects requiring primary-source testing:

- S&A Concrete.
- Anthony “Fat Tony” Salerno.
- Paul Castellano.
- Teamsters Local 282.
- John Cody.
- Roy Cohn.
- The allegation that an apartment or condominium benefit was provided to a Cody-associated person during the Trump Tower construction period.

These names should be used as search identifiers, not as proof that Trump participated in a criminal enterprise. The strongest records would be FBI reports, grand-jury-derived public filings, trial exhibits, subpoenas, property instruments, payment records, or final agency summaries.

## Research questions that can be answered by records

1. Which legal entities acquired, financed, constructed, owned, or sold interests in the Grand Hyatt and Trump Tower?
2. Which banks, insurers, title companies, law firms, contractors, unions, and public agencies appear repeatedly across the transactions?
3. Did federal investigators create records concerning Trump Tower construction, Local 282, S&A Concrete, John Cody, or a related property transfer?
4. Did any investigative record identify a Trump-affiliated property, unit, company, attorney, address, or financial intermediary as part of an asset-tracing inquiry?
5. Do the names and identifiers in these records intersect with independently documented narcotics or financial investigations from the same period?
6. Are apparent intersections exact matches, probable matches, or merely similar names?

## Recommended record-acquisition sequence

### Step 1 — Build the property spine

Use New York City's Automated City Register Information System (ACRIS) to obtain recorded documents for Manhattan properties. ACRIS includes deeds, mortgages, leases, assignments, satisfactions, UCC-related instruments, federal tax liens, and other recorded documents for Manhattan from 1966 forward.

Official source: [NYC Department of Finance — Property Records](https://www.nyc.gov/site/finance/property/property-recording-property-related-documents.page)

For each relevant parcel or condominium unit, capture:

- Borough, block, and lot.
- Document and transaction numbers.
- Grantor and grantee names exactly as recorded.
- Entity type and jurisdiction.
- Consideration and mortgage amount.
- Recording and execution dates.
- Lender, title company, attorneys, return address, and managing agent.
- Assignments, satisfactions, liens, and subsequent transfers.

The result should be a chronological transaction table before any laundering inference is made.

### Step 2 — Obtain court records before broad FOIA requests

FOIA does not apply to federal courts. Locate relevant federal cases through PACER and obtain older files through the court clerk or National Archives when appropriate.

Official sources:

- [PACER — Find a Case](https://pacer.uscourts.gov/find-case)
- [National Archives — Court Records](https://www.archives.gov/research/court-records)

PACER notes that pre-November 1, 2004 criminal documents may not be electronically available to nonparties. For older prosecutions, request the docket and complete case file from the clerk or the appropriate National Archives facility.

Prioritize:

- Criminal and civil cases involving S&A Concrete, Local 282, John Cody, Anthony Salerno, or Paul Castellano.
- Labor-racketeering or extortion prosecutions that generated Trump Tower exhibits or testimony.
- Property, forfeiture, tax, or receivership proceedings that identify relevant real estate or project entities.

Request or inspect:

- Docket sheets.
- Indictments and superseding indictments.
- Search-warrant materials, where unsealed.
- Exhibit and witness lists.
- Trial and sentencing exhibits.
- Plea and sentencing memoranda.
- Forfeiture schedules.
- Transcripts mentioning Trump Tower, Trump Tower Associates, S&A Concrete, Local 282, John Cody, Roy Cohn, or an apartment transfer.

### Step 3 — File an event-centered FBI request

The FBI permits FOIA requests concerning organizations, businesses, investigations, historical events, incidents, groups, and deceased persons. An event-centered request is preferable to a demand for a living person's complete FBI file, which creates substantial third-party privacy problems.

Official source: [FBI — Requesting FBI Records](https://www.fbi.gov/how-we-can-help-you/more-fbi-services-and-information/freedom-of-information-privacy-act/requesting-fbi-records)

Recommended caption:

> Trump Tower construction, Teamsters Local 282, S&A Concrete, and John Cody-related transactions, 1980–1984

Search identifiers:

- Trump Tower, 721 Fifth Avenue, New York, NY.
- Trump Tower Associates.
- S&A Concrete.
- Teamsters Local 282.
- John Cody.
- Roy M. Cohn.
- Anthony Salerno.
- Paul Castellano.
- Bonwit Teller demolition and Trump Tower construction.
- Any Trump Tower condominium or apartment allegedly supplied to a Cody-associated person.

Requested record categories:

1. Final investigative and closing summaries.
2. FD-302 interview reports where releasable.
3. Investigative memoranda, airtels, electronic communications, and referrals.
4. Existing file numbers, classifications, subfiles, and cross-references.
5. Subpoenas and document requests that are no longer protected by grand-jury secrecy, or records describing their non-secret administrative handling.
6. Communications with the Department of Labor, IRS, a United States Attorney's Office, New York law-enforcement agencies, or prosecutors handling labor-racketeering matters.
7. Records concerning the ownership, transfer, occupancy, financing, or use of a Trump Tower unit in connection with the investigation.
8. Records concerning contractor payments, labor peace, extortion, racketeering, bribery, or concealed benefits associated with the construction project.

### Step 4 — Request public-agency transaction records

Depending on the custodian, use New York's Freedom of Information Law (FOIL) for state and local executive-agency records. Potential custodians include:

- Empire State Development or the successor custodian for Urban Development Corporation records.
- New York City Department of Finance.
- New York City Department of Housing Preservation and Development.
- New York City Department of Buildings.
- New York City Economic Development Corporation, where it maintains responsive public records.
- New York State Attorney General, limited to records not protected by law-enforcement or attorney exemptions.

Potential Grand Hyatt record categories:

- Board minutes and resolutions.
- Lease and lease amendments.
- Tax-abatement applications and calculations.
- Appraisals, feasibility studies, and financial projections.
- Correspondence with Trump entities, Hyatt entities, lenders, and counsel.
- Default, audit, revenue-sharing, or compliance records.

Potential Trump Tower record categories:

- Building permits and amendments.
- Demolition and safety records.
- Contractor and subcontractor disclosures in agency files.
- Zoning, air-rights, landmark, and public-space records.
- Condominium offering-plan filings and amendments held by the Attorney General.

## Cross-reference protocol

Every extracted identifier should be normalized into a research table with these fields:

| Field | Required treatment |
|---|---|
| Person | Full name, aliases, date of birth where lawfully public |
| Entity | Exact legal name, jurisdiction, formation date, registered agent |
| Property | Address, unit, borough-block-lot, document number |
| Financial institution | Exact name at transaction date; successor institution separately noted |
| Professional intermediary | Attorney, accountant, broker, title company, managing agent |
| Communication | Telephone number, address, telex, cable, or email, as applicable to period |
| Government record | Agency, office, file number, case number, document date, page |
| Match quality | Exact, probable, possible, or rejected |
| Evidentiary status | Allegation, sworn testimony, agency finding, judicial finding, conviction, acquittal, or unresolved |

### Match rules

- **Exact match:** the same unique identifier appears in two independent primary records.
- **Probable match:** multiple identifiers agree, but a unique identifier is absent.
- **Possible match:** only a name or general location agrees.
- **Rejected match:** dates, addresses, middle names, ownership records, or other identifiers demonstrate different people or entities.

Do not promote a possible match to a factual connection without corroboration.

## What would materially strengthen the research

A meaningful evidentiary intersection would include one or more of the following:

- A federal investigative report naming a Trump-affiliated entity and a specific transaction.
- A property document matching a person, company, address, or intermediary in an independent criminal asset investigation.
- A court exhibit or sworn testimony identifying a payment, property benefit, nominee, or concealed ownership arrangement.
- An FBI cross-reference linking a Trump Tower-related file to a separate organized-crime or narcotics investigation.
- A final regulator or agency finding, rather than a press characterization.

## What would not establish the claim

The following are insufficient by themselves:

- Temporal overlap.
- Presence in the same city or building.
- A similar surname.
- A later criminal conviction unrelated to the transaction being studied.
- A journalist's inference not supported by the cited underlying document.
- An indictment treated as though it were a conviction or judicial finding.

## Status

This tranche is a records-acquisition roadmap. It identifies testable hypotheses and does **not** presently establish that proceeds from Cuban or other narcotics trafficking entered the Grand Hyatt, Trump Tower, or another Trump property during 1977–1983.
