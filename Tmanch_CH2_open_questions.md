# Chapter 2 Open Questions and Records Plan

## Purpose

This file converts Chapter 2’s unresolved hypotheses into specific archival, court, property, regulatory, and FOIA assignments. Each program identifies the decisive question, likely custodian, search identifiers, evidence threshold, and draft request language.

Priority scale:

- **P1:** capable of materially changing the chapter’s central conclusion;
- **P2:** capable of strengthening a major supporting mechanism;
- **P3:** useful corroboration or chronology.

# Success standard

A successful Chapter 2 records program should answer four distinct questions:

1. **Selection:** Why did Soviet officials choose Trump?
2. **Operation:** What did they do during and after the approach?
3. **Effect:** What changed in Trump’s conduct, messaging, business, or political activity?
4. **Leverage:** What financial, criminal, reputational, or personal vulnerabilities were identified or usable?

The search should not require all four answers to prove one giant conspiracy. Each channel should be documented independently and then compared through chronology, shared actors, communications, and financial records.
